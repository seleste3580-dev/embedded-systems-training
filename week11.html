Week 11 - Capstone Project Component Exploration

Objectives

The main objectives of the eleventh week of the embedded systems training program were to:

  • Finalize the idea and scope for the final capstone project.
  • Identify and gather all necessary hardware components required for the project.
  • Perform an in-depth review of the datasheets for each chosen component.
  • Understand the power, communication, and pinout requirements to prepare for prototyping.

Overview

Key activities from Week 11:

  • Dedicated significant time to reading and analyzing datasheets for sensors, microcontrollers (ESP32), and actuators.
  • Mapped out the system architecture and created initial block diagrams of the capstone project.
  • Planned out the communication interfaces (e.g., I2C, SPI, UART) needed between the central microcontroller and the peripherals.
  • Assessed power consumption and voltage levels to ensure safe and reliable hardware integration.

Resources and Links

Below are essential resources and references utilized during this week's planning phase:

  • Electronic Components Datasheet Search
  • System architecture and block diagram drafts (Internal documentation).

Overall, Week 11 laid the critical theoretical and hardware foundation required before diving into the final project development.
